Project Team #9: Environment, DS4200 F20

Derek Brenner, Julia Nitschke, Samyak Jain

Service-Learning Course Project as part of DS 4200 F20: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Cody Dunne, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

This project is about the relationship between economic behavior, education, and outcomes, and particularly their relation to environmental concern. The idea that people should become more aware of environmental issues is not sufficient; we need pro-environmental action. We are particularly interested in what kind of information impacts people's perceptions of their actions, and the the trigger points that make people act on their knowledge and awareness. Our partner is a research team at Northeastern led by economics professor Dr. Venkatesan. The data used in this project is from a survey conducted by Dr. Venkatesan and a group of students.

Visualization explanation

Link presentation slides!

Our visualization looks at public reycling data and recycling/enviornmental action data gained from a survey. The visualization shows that only around 8.5% of plastics were recycled in 2018. Many survey respondents recycle and think that the percentage of recycled items is much higher than the actual percentage. We look at the relationship between recycling habits and the perception of the problem of climate change. Additionally, we relate this to the action element of willingness to pay additional money to eliminate enviornmental damage of single-use cups.
